About Our Hospital

Tikur Anbessa, or the Black Lion,

is a significant cultural and historical symbol of Ethiopia.
The Black Lion represents strength, courage, and the proud heritage of the Ethiopian people.
In Ethiopian culture, the lion has been revered as a royal symbol, often associated with the monarchy,
particularly the ruling dynasty of Emperor Haile Selassie.


Tikur Anbessa is also an important figure in Ethiopian mythology and history,
symbolizing national unity and resilience in the face of adversity.
Its image is often used in art, literature, and even the national flag of Ethiopia.


Whether in ancient texts or modern-day references,
Tikur Anbessa continues to embody the spirit of Ethiopia,
honoring its rich traditions and enduring pride.
